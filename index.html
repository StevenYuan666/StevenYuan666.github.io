Haolun Wu

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at McGill University and Mila - Quebec AI Institute. I am delighted in working with Steve Liu and Fernando Diaz. I also collaborate with Laurent Charlin and Joelle Pineau, and have them on my supervisory committee.

My research is around human-centric AI and broadly touches personalization, evaluation, and responsibility in information access systems and LLMs. I am specifically interested in learning from human feedback for personalization as well as social benefits. Additionally, I very much love interdisciplinary collaboration and am particularly interested in applying AI/ML techniques to Education and Psychology [CHI'24] A summary of the topics covered in some of my projects that I led or highly engaged is listed below:

  • User modeling and preference elicitation [NeurIPS'24] [ICDE'24] [TOIS'23]
  • Data-driven personalized recommendation [CIKM'24] [CIKM'22] [AAAI'21]
  • Efficient knowledge discovery [EMNLP'24] [ICLR'24]
  • Alignment to human value and social good (e.g., fairness, diversity, transparency, etc.) [NeurIPS'24] [TKDE'24] [SIGIR ICTIR'24] [SIGIR'22] [TOIS'22]
  • LLMs for personalization, RLHF with personalization, ...

During my Ph.D. journey, I interned and collaborated closely with Google Research and Microsoft Research. My research is generously supported by McGill Graduate Excellence Award, Borealis AI Fellowship, and FRQNT PhD Scholarship (ranked 1st place).
